If you're caring for an aging parent, someone has probably suggested Life360. It's the go-to family safety app for millions of people. But was it designed for seniors living independently?

Let's compare Life360 and EverWhen side-by-side to help you decide which approach is right for your family.

---

## The Core Difference

**Life360** is built around real-time location sharing. Everyone in the family shares their GPS location at all times. You can see where your parent is right now, where they've been, and how fast they drove there.

**EverWhen** is built around daily check-ins. Your parent tells you they're okay each morning with a single tap. No tracking. No GPS. No always-on monitoring.

These aren't just different features — they represent fundamentally different philosophies about senior safety.

---

## What Seniors Actually Think

Here's what we hear from families most often:

> "I suggested Life360 to my mom. She said, 'I'm 76 years old. I don't need you watching me like I'm a teenager out past curfew.'"

This reaction is more common than you might think. A study by the Pew Research Center found that **70% of adults 65+ say it's important to them that they can go about their daily lives without being tracked.**

The problem isn't the technology — it's the message it sends. Always-on location tracking tells your parent: "I don't trust you to take care of yourself."

A daily check-in says something different: "I care about you, and I want to know you're okay."

## When Life360 Makes Sense

Life360 is a great product for the right use case:

- Active families with teens driving
- Parents who travel frequently and want to share location with kids
- Seniors who drive a lot and want crash detection
- Families where everyone wants mutual tracking (it goes both ways)

---

## When EverWhen Makes Sense

EverWhen is built specifically for a different scenario:

- **Seniors living independently at home** — the primary safety concern is "are they okay today?"
- **Parents who value privacy** — they don't want to be tracked, and you should respect that
- **Families managing medications** — daily med reminders are built in
- **Long-distance caregivers** — you can't check in person, but you need to know they're okay
- **Seniors with chronic conditions** — glucose tracking and wellness monitoring

## The Battery Problem

One thing many families don't consider: Life360's constant GPS tracking is hard on phone batteries. For a senior whose phone might already struggle to last a full day, that's a real problem.

If your parent's phone dies at 2 PM because Life360 drained it, you lose the safety net entirely. Worse — you see their location drop off the map, and you don't know why. Cue the panic.

EverWhen uses virtually no battery because there's no GPS, no background tracking, no constant connection. One notification, one tap, done for the day.

---

## The Acceptance Problem

The most effective safety solution is the one your parent actually uses.

If you install Life360 and your parent feels surveilled, they'll uninstall it. Or they'll "forget" to charge their phone. Or they'll leave it at home on purpose. We've heard all of these stories.

EverWhen's daily check-in is something seniors actually agree to — and often like. It's not surveillance; it's a morning routine. Many seniors tell us they appreciate the medication reminders and the feeling that someone cares enough to check in, without the invasiveness of being watched.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes! Some families use Life360 for real-time location (with the senior's consent) and EverWhen for daily wellness checks and medication management. They serve different purposes and work well together.

But if your parent says no to location tracking — don't push it. EverWhen gives you the "are they okay?" answer without the tracking debate.
